Frequently Asked Questions About Employer Compliance

What are the most common pregnancy discrimination violations by employers?

Common violations include refusing reasonable accommodations, retaliating against employees who request accommodations, failing to provide equal treatment, and not having compliant leave policies. Many employers also make discriminatory comments or decisions based on assumptions about pregnant employees' capabilities.

What does the Pregnant Workers Fairness Act require from employers?

The PWFA requires employers with 15+ employees to provide reasonable accommodations for known limitations related to pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions, unless it causes undue hardship. This includes modifications to work duties, schedules, or workplace conditions.

How much does pregnancy discrimination legal consultation cost?

Our initial employer consultation is free. Ongoing compliance services are customized based on your organization's size and needs. The cost is significantly less than defending a single discrimination lawsuit, which averages $200,000+ in legal fees alone.

What size companies need pregnancy discrimination compliance help?

While federal law applies to employers with 15+ employees, Human Rights Law covers employers with 4+ employees. Even smaller employers benefit from compliant policies to prevent costly litigation and create positive workplace culture.
